Everyday at The Best Restaurant Around we have a special dish that is even more special than the specialties we serve here. We try to take as many flavors as possible and create a twist on a old favorite. We also provide the recipe to show the method to our madness.


⧏ Today's Special: Whiskey and Triple Pork Black Bean Chili ⧐


Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork shoulder, cut into 1 - ½ inch chunks
  • Vegetable oil for a lean pork shoulder only
  • 5 ½ lb slices of thick-cut applewood smoked bacon, chopped
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 ½ cups dried black beans, cleaned of debris and rinsed
  • 2 chopped canned chipotle chiles
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 qt. reduced-sodium chicken broth
  • ¾ cup whiskey such as Jack Daniel's, divided
  • 4 ounces Spanish smoked chorizo, quartered lengthwise and thinly sliced crosswise
  • ½ cup coarsely chopped flat-leaf parsley
  • Crème fraîche or shredded jack cheese
  • Chopped chives

Available in small, medium, or large sized bowl. Also available as a sandwich topping or entree side for additional cost.


Instructions to Make

  1. Brown pork in a large pot over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, about 10 minutes, adding a little oil if pork is very lean.
  2. Transfer pork to a plate. Add bacon to pot and cook, stirring often, until browned but still soft, about 5 minutes.
  3. Spoon out fat and return 2 tbsp. to pot.
  4. Add onion and celery and cook, stirring often, until onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.
  5. Return pork to pot and add beans, chipotles, cumin, broth, and ½ cup whiskey.
  6. Cover and bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat and simmer until beans and pork are tender, about 1 ¼ hours.
  7. Stir in chorizo and remaining ¼ cup whiskey. Simmer, covered, until flavors are blended, about 10 minutes more.
  8. Stir in parsley.
  9. Ladle into bowls and top with crème fraîche and chives.
